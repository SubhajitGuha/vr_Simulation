

SUBPARIETAL SULCUS


The subparietal sulcus (Sulcus subparietalis) or suprasplenial sulcus is a sulcus, or crevice, on the medial surface of each cerebral hemisphere, above the splenium of the corpus callosum. 

It separates the precuneus from the posterior part of the cingulate gyrus. 

It is the posterior continuation of the cingulate sulcus. 

The cingulate sulcus actually "terminates" as the marginal sulcus of the cingulate sulcus (margin of cingulate gyrus). 

It extends posteriorly toward the calcarine sulcus.

The precuneus is bordered anteriorly by the marginal branch of the cingulate sulcus (margin of cingulate sulcus), posteriorly by the parietooccipital sulcus, and inferiorly by the subparietal sulcus.
